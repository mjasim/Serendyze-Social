Objective and Tutorial

This tutorial introduces the study objectives and various functionalities of this tool called DICE. You can access this tutorial anytime from the navigation bar.

Refreshing the page will result in losing all progress. Please do not refresh the page unless you want to start over.

Study Objective:

In this step of the study you will thoroughly explore the product reviews from 3 headphones and decide which particular headphone you would refer to someone. After you have made the final decision, click the decision button at the top right of the navigation bar. A post study questionnaire will follow that will ask you a few questions about your decision-making process. Once you have answered all questions, proceed to M-Turk and finish the study.

Tutorial

Selecting a Product:

Click on the dropdown to select a product to explore. You can switch between the products at any time.

Interface Features:

The interface has the following features:

  • Keywords: These are the most frequent keyword pairs that are present in the reviews.
  • Sentiments: These categories represent different sentiments present in the reviews.
  • Exploration metrics: These metrics (Visit, Coverage, and Distribution) provide you with tracking information based on your exploration of the review reviews.
    Visit information shows the amount of reviews you have interacted with directly by clicking or hovering over them for 3 seconds.
    Coverage information shows the amount of reviews you have covered implicitly, including the reviews that are very similar and redundant to the ones you have previously interacted with.
    Distribution information shows the proportion of review sentiments you have directly interacted with.
  • List of reviews: The list provides the reviews along with their associated sentiments.
  • List of suggested reviews that you may find useful: The list provides the reviews suggested based on your interaction with other reviews.
Filtering reviews:

Select the keywords and sentiments to filter the review reviews accordingly. You can use a keyword pair and a sentiment at the same time. If you select keyword pair, they will be highlighted in the reviews where they appear.

You can also use the search option to search for any keyword of your choice. The keyword you searched will be highlighted in the reviews where they appear.

Interacting with the reviews:

You can select or hover on a review for a while to mark it as read. DICE will keep a record of your progress and track the reviews you are interacting with.

Exploration metrics:

The exploration metric bars provide information on how you have been exploring the reviews. The are three metrics bars.

Visit: Visit presents the number of reviews you have read and marked as read by clicking or hovering on it for more than 3 seconds. It essentially tells you how many reviews you have already looked at. In the example below, you have read 28% reviews.

Coverage: Coverage presents the amount of reviews you have knowledge about based on your interactions with reviews. Oftentimes, there are reviews that are similar and redundant to the reviews you have already read. Essentially, it tells you how many redundant reviews you implicitly have knowledge on. In the example below, you have covered 45% reviews despite visiting or reading 28% reviews. The extra 17% (45% - 28%) reviews are similar and redundant to the reviews you have read already.

Distribution: Distribution presents what kind of sentiments you have been exploring. It essentially tells you whether you are focusing on reviews with similar sentiments or your are exploring all sentiments comprehensively. In the example below, you have read 12% positive and 11% neutral reviews but covered 32% negative reviews.

Hovering over Visit or Coverage metric bars will show the review exploration progress corresponding to the most frequent terms and sentiments.

Clicking on Visit or Coverage metric bars will filter the reviews and only show the review associated with your selection. In the following example, only the Coverage metric bar is selected. The reviews are filtered accordingly and only reviews that are covered are shown.

Suggested reviews that you may find useful

Depending on your exploration history (the reviews you have been interacting with, DICE will provide you with suggested reviews from the the reviews that you have not visited yet. You can use the checkbox beside the product selection dropdown to toggle the suggestions on or off. You can also interact with the suggested reviews similar to regular reviews. You can click or hover on the suggested reviews for 3 seconds to mark them as read. Once you interact with regular reviews or suggested reviews with the suggested reviews window open, DICE will provide you with newly suggested reviews based on your interactions. Suggesting reviews takes some time. Please wait until the wait spinner () stops moving completely before marking another review as read to get new suggestions.

The suggested reviews you interacted with already are displayed below the new suggested reviews in reverse-chronological order, the review you interacted with last, will show up at the top.
